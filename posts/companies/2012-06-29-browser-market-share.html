The Times They Are a-Changin'

The high-tech industry has always been a tumultuous place. Companies come and go. Today's leaders become tomorrow's losers. Companies boom then bust only to boom again. At least to me, it seems that changes are coming faster and faster.

While I have no insight into what Bob Dylan was thinking when he wrote the song, clearly the times they are a-changin'.

Have there been many recent drastic changes? I think so but I'll let you decide. Below is an ideosynchratic collection of facts dating back to 2004.

Microsoft Rules

2007

Microsoft lost an antitrust suit in the US. Like the European antitrust suit, this focused on how Microsoft was using its dominance of desktop computers to force its web browser, Internet Explorer, on its customers.

2008

Microsoft loses an antitrust suit in Europe. Like the American antitrust suit, this focused on how Microsoft was using its dominance of desktop computers to force its web browser, Internet Explorer, on its customers. Concidentally, earlier this week Microsoft exhausted its appeals and will have to pay the billion dollar fine from the European suit.

Present Day

Microsoft TV is AWOL.

2005 September

Apple launches its first iPod Touch and the world will never be the same.

2007 January

Apple launches Apple TV.

2007 June

The original iPhone is launched.

2004 February

Facebook was founded.

2004 August

Google had its now famous IPO.

2010 April

Apple releases iPad.

Social Websites Rise

2006 July

Twitter was born. Since then Twitter, accurately or not, has been credited for major political changes such as the so-called Arab Spring. Currently, Twitter is being used by the Colorado Springs Fire Department to provide residents timely updates of the large fires in the area. At the time of writing this, @CSFDPIO has 3,700 followers and 16 tweets in less than 2 hours.

2012 Mid-May

Facebook's frequently, maligned by the press IPO has seen the company's market capitalization drop by $21 billion from its initial market capitalization of over $90 billion dollars. While doubtlessly upsetting for investors, Facebook is still worth about $70 billion dollars.

Internet Everywhere

2004

13% of the world's population have internet access.

2010

28% of the world's population have internet access.

2009 November

94% of people living in the US have broadband internet access at home. 99% of people in the US have broadband internet access at work.
