Bookifying News

I frequently find it convenient to take a website on the road. That is, I want a website or part of a website in a convenient, portable, easily accessible format on my Asus Transformer tablet. It has WiFi but no mobile data. Without a portable means of accessing a website, the lack of data plan would dramatically limit the usability of my tablet.

I have taken to bookifying websites that I have an interest in. For most sites, I convert the newest data on the site into an epub book, load it onto my tablet, and then read it using one an ebook reader.

Besides bookifying the newest articles on LifeHacker, I have also bookified entire websites.

The cool part is, this process requires a one-time configuration and then works automatically. That is, the entire web sites sit on my tablet and old website articles are automatically replaced by the new ones.

The Process

The bookification process utilizes the following tools:

  • The FOSS epub creation and library management tool Calibre.
  • The Cheetah Sync file-synchronization application for Android.
  • The Moon+ Android book reader.

Calibre comes packed with features for downloading new postings on a website, converting them to an epub book, and then managing them in a library. It has nice extras such as updating sites on a customizable schedule and allowing you to specify how many epub collections of a site you wish to keep.

Cheetah Sync provides WiFi folder synchronization between your desktop (Mac OS X or Windows) and your Android tablet. This synchronization can happen on a schedule or manually to reduce battery usage.

Moon+ is one af a large number of Android book readers. I like it as it seems to facilitate being used in this manner. It does a good job of monitoring a directory on the file system for changes.

Books And Websites

Besides using Calibre to create epub books, it also does a good job of managing existing ones. For example, SapphireSteel Software, whose name is an esoteric reference to a late 1970's British TV show, have published an epub book called The Little Book Of Ruby. I'm slowly learning Ruby and sometimes it is nice to be able to look up something when I'm not near a WiFi connection and don't want to use my mobile WiFi hotspot as that tends to get costly quickly.

This strategy also provides a means for bookifying entire websites or a specified portion of them. Calibre comes with a utility app called web2disk that can download a website to your local disk. Once there, Calibre can convert it to an epub book. There are many sites, such as this one, that cover the details of this conversion.

While web2disk seems like a fine program, I'm very familiar with the Linux program curl(1) and tend to use that to download an entire website. This Google search has numerous references on using curl to download a website. curl seems to be more flexible but also more complicated.

While Moon+ is my preference for ebook news as responds well to files being added or deleted by an external program, Calibre and Cheetah Sync in my case, I prefer uploading my books to Amazon's Kindle library. I find that the Kindle is particularly good at synchronizing bookmarks and notes. I use the Kindle for Android app on my tablet and smartphone and the Kindle for Desktop or browser from my computer.
